About

This Plagiarism Detection platform is the result of years of studies on music plagiarism and music similarities fields, conducted by researchers and students at the Musimathics Lab of the University of Salerno.

Plagiarism, i.e., the act of copying the work of others and trying to pass it off as one own is seen as a legal offense. In the Internet era, the fast, vast, and easy access of information has further escalated the problem of plagiarism. Plagiarism occurs when two works are “substantially similar”, whereas the owner of one of the two works has "copied" the other. Interpreting or measuring the concept of substantially similarity is actually an open issue.

According to the legislation, there are no general and accepted rules according to which a minimum number of notes, or beats, simultaneously present in two music compositions constitutes plagiarism. Over the years has emerged the need of defining a general criteria to identify cases of plagiarism. Even if not emphasized by the law, melody usually is the deciding element in cases of plagiarism. In fact, often we talk about melodic plagiarism to indicate music plagiarism. It is a phenomenon very debated in the music context, particularly in the context of western pop music.
The recognition of a music plagiarism is not a trivial task, because it depends on the ability of recognizing similarities between melodic fragments inside two melodies. We have proposed both text-based and fuzzy-based plagiarism detection tools. This Plagiarism Detection platform embeds a metric of similarity that uses text similarity and clustering techniques.
